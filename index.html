The Detergent Crisis

Conventional detergents significantly pollute our water systems due to high levels of phosphate compounds. These compounds trigger an unnatural explosion in phytoplankton populations (severe algae blooms).

This excessive algae blocks sunlight and heavily depletes the dissolved oxygen supply in rivers, disrupting the respiratory processes of local fish species, causing mass mortality and threatening overall aquatic biodiversity.

Nature's Cleaning Power

Alcazyme is a revolutionary, 100% biodegradable liquid detergent. It utilizes natural plant saponins from Aloe vera for cleansing and fermented organic waste (Eco Enzyme) to tackle stubborn stains while actively preserving our vital aquatic ecosystems.

The addition of locally-sourced Cananga flowers introduces a premium natural aroma, solving the typical "unpleasant smell" issue of natural detergents without using synthetic chemicals.

The Magic of Organic Fermentation

What is Eco Enzyme?

Eco Enzyme is a multifunctional liquid produced from the fermentation of organic kitchen waste (such as fruit and vegetable peels), brown sugar (molasses), and water. This liquid is the primary key to Alcazyme's cleaning power.

  • Production Process (1:3:10 Ratio)

    Anaerobically fermented for 3 months away from sunlight. Uses the golden ratio: 1 part brown sugar, 3 parts fruit peels (orange, pineapple, watermelon, melon, dragon fruit), and 10 parts clean water.

  • Functions & Active Compounds

    Highly rich in amylase, protease, and lipase enzymes that actively break down stubborn organic stains (fat, protein, starch). Doubles as liquid soap, disinfectant, and even a purifier for polluted rivers.

  • Maximum Benefits

    Highly economical, made from safe natural ingredients, and directly helps reduce the volume of household organic waste ending up in landfills.

Gentle Natural Surfactant

Aloe Vera

Aloe vera (Aloe barbadensis miller) is a thick, fleshy succulent plant long known for its rich benefits. In Alcazyme, this plant plays a crucial role as a natural cleaning agent replacing harsh chemicals.

Availability & Composition

Abundant and easy to cultivate in tropical climates like Indonesia. Rich in natural saponins (foam-forming compounds), lignin, enzymes, as well as various vitamins and minerals.

Primary Functions

Generally widely used as a natural moisturizer, mild burn healer, natural antibacterial, and base ingredient in the cosmetics industry.

Function in Alcazyme

The saponin content acts as a natural surfactant that produces foam to bind dirt particles on the fabric. Meanwhile, lignin helps maintain the softness of the user's hands during manual washing.

Traditional Aromatherapy

Cananga Flowers

Cananga flowers (Cananga odorata) are traditional tropical greenish-yellow flowers known for their intense and soothing fragrance. These flowers are the key differentiating element of Alcazyme from other natural detergents.

Availability & Composition

Grows wildly and is widely cultivated locally in Indonesia, including the Banjarmasin area. Contains high essential oils, linalool, and benzyl acetate.

Primary Functions

Traditionally used for aromatherapy, stress relaxation, cultural rituals, and even exported as a base ingredient for world-class perfumes.

Function in Alcazyme

Acts as a natural aromatic agent (fragrance) that completely replaces toxic synthetic perfumes. This formulation provides a fresh, long-lasting scent that clings to fabric fibers while being respiratory-friendly.

Processing Method

How Alcazyme is Made

1

Eco Enzyme Fermentation

We utilize discarded fruit peels (orange, pineapple, watermelon, dragon fruit) sourced locally. A 3-month anaerobic fermentation process is used with a strict ratio of 1 part brown sugar : 3 parts fruit peels : 10 parts clean water.

2

Extraction

100g of Aloe vera is peeled and mashed to extract the core gel. 100g of Cananga flowers are boiled for 3 minutes to produce a fragrant natural extract.

3

Formulation

The cooled Cananga extract is blended with 100g of Aloe vera gel, 100ml of aged Eco Enzyme, and 4g of baking soda until a homogenous, aromatic detergent is formed.

Beyond Cleaning

Sustainability & Product Impact

A comprehensive approach to environmental preservation, community empowerment, and scalable eco-friendly entrepreneurship.

01 / 06

Product Sustainability

Alcazyme supports sustainable household practices by utilizing biodegradable plant-based ingredients and recycled organic waste through eco enzyme fermentation. This approach reduces chemical pollution and encourages circular waste management in communities.

02 / 06

Community Implementation

Alcazyme can be produced through community-based workshops where residents learn how to ferment organic waste into eco enzyme and combine it with natural plant extracts to create an environmentally friendly detergent.

03 / 06

Ease of Production

The production process of Alcazyme is simple and does not require industrial equipment. Most materials such as aloe vera, cananga flowers, and fruit peels are commonly available in tropical regions like Indonesia.

04 / 06

Economic Potential

Alcazyme has potential as a small-scale eco-friendly business product. With low production costs and increasing public awareness of environmental issues, natural detergents have a growing market for home industries (UMKM).

05 / 06

Environmental Impact

By replacing synthetic surfactants with natural saponins, Alcazyme actively reduces water pollution, safeguards aquatic organisms, and promotes a holistic green lifestyle while minimizing household organic waste.

06 / 06

Future Development

Future plans include testing against a wider variety of stubborn stains, machine-wash compatibility, exploring natural aroma variations, utilizing eco-friendly packaging, and scaling up production for mass distribution.
